How to Hold in Pee when You Can't Use the Bathroom
沒有廁所的時候怎麼憋尿

Having to hold in pee may be difficult or unpleasant. But if you are without acceptable facilities, privacy, time, or opportunity; or if you're not allowed to use the restroom - there are ways to cope.
情況不允許尿尿可能是個困難又讓人不開心的事情。但是，如果設施，隱私、時間、或時機上都不允許，又或者如果人家不准你使用廁所，其實還是有辦法可想的。

Steps 步驟
1.        Close your urethra inward. Most people do this automatically when a slight urge to pee is first felt. As the urge to pee grows, tensing unnecessary muscles can waste effort and may make the problem worse. Focus instead on isolating and squeezing muscles surrounding the urethra while allowing other muscles to relax on their own. Use the genito-urinary muscles to exert closing tension at the pee outlet. Relax the pelvis and abdomen to avoid inner pressure on the bladder.
從內部關緊您的尿道。 大多數人在感覺到輕微的尿意時就會這麼做了。 當尿意增長，拉緊不必要的的肌肉不但浪費不必要的精力和可能使問題變得更糟。重點應該放在擠壓尿道周圍的肌肉，同時放鬆其他肌肉。 拉緊泌尿生殖附近的肌肉以關閉尿道出口。 放鬆骨盆和腹部避免對膀胱產生的內在壓力。
2.        Consider if you are standing, sitting, or moving.  考慮您是否站立，坐或者移動
o        Cross your legs when standing. Don't cross your legs when sitting. When standing, crossing your legs helps you "hold it in" by compressing the urethra, surrounding tissues, and (in females) the genito-urinary opening. However, when sitting any position which raises one or both thighs towards your abdomen causes pressure on the bladder - making it harder to holding it.
當站立時，交叉您的腿。 當坐時，不要交叉您的腿。當站著的時候，交叉雙腿有助您透過壓縮尿道、周圍的組織和(女性)泌尿生殖器而不讓尿道開啟而能忍住。 然而，當坐著時翹起任何一腿會往您的腹部和膀胱施加壓力—更難忍住。
o        Sit upright but relaxed. Don't "slouch". Raise your upper-body and allow you back to arch taking pressure off your bladder, but do not "stretch" your abdomen as this will add pressure. Relax your abdomen.
Position your legs what ever way is most comfortable. Don't cross your legs above the knee. If you cross your legs, cross them at the ankles or shins. Press your thighs together, but avoid over-tensing them. Let your pelvis roll or tilt to ease your bladder.

Do not lean forward, pull the front of your pelvis up, or squeeze your abdomen inward.
挺直坐，但是放輕鬆。「不要懶散」。坐直您的上半身，可以讓你減輕您的膀胱的壓力，但是「不要拉伸」您的腹部。 放鬆您的腹部。
用最舒適的方式安置您的腿。 不要蹺腳。 如果要的話，跨在腳踝或小腿上就好。 把大腿按緊，但是避免在用力過渡。 骨盆有時可以扭動一下緩和您的膀胱。
不要前傾，拉前面的骨盆，或者你的腹部向內擠壓。
o        Stand using your legs to close your urinary structures, either by squeezing or pulling tight. Choose positions you can keep your balance in naturally without tensing. Relax the rest of your lower body. Reposition your legs and thighs as needed.
使用您的雙腿站立關閉泌尿系統。不管是擠壓或是拉緊，選擇能保持自然平衡不受壓力的姿勢。放鬆其餘的下半身。 需要的話調整大腿小腿的位置。
o        Allow your lower abdomen to tilt slightly forwards, if it does so on its own. This may relieve tension from your bladder.
讓您的下腹部稍微向前傾斜，讓它自然前傾。這可能可以緩和膀胱的緊張。
Allow the small of your back to arch so your chest and head remain upright.
Let your abdomen expand and your front pelvis "tip" downwards (raising the buttocks).
Don't assume this position unless it happens naturally, standing this way deliberately can become awkward and create tension.
小小的拱身，卻可以讓您的胸口和頭保持挺直。讓你和你的腹部擴大，前骨盆下降（提高臀部）。不要故意這麼做，除非已經到了沒辦法的地步。這樣站著會顯得笨拙而且造成緊張。
3.        Avoid jarring or shaking your body. 避免震動或搖晃你的身體。
o        Stay sitting, if sitting. Remain standing, if standing. Unless it achieves greater comfort. Use smooth and careful movements if you do sit or stand (ex., when standing after a long movie). Avoid changing positions too suddenly.
繼續坐，如果坐。 繼續站立，如果站立。 除非下一個姿勢讓自己更舒服。緩慢和小心的移動 例如看完一部長的電影以後要站起來)時。 避免太突然改變姿勢。
o        Keep movements as fluid and graceful as possible when you walk or perform other activities. 走路或從事其他活動時盡可能保持運動的流暢和優雅
o        If you notice your hips tend to swing more as you walk let them do so. It allows the pelvic floor muscle to remain tightened. But don't exaggerate this movement if it shakes your bladder.
如果您發現您的臀部往往在走路的時候會搖晃。 使骨盆底肌繼續收緊。 但是，不要誇大這一運動以免搖晃你的膀胱。
o        Avoid tense, abrupt, or sudden movement. Remain smooth and natural.
It actually makes it worse to "run to the bathroom" when you can go.
Walk with strong steps, do not run.
避免緊張，突然，或突然移動。保持流暢和自然。 「跑向廁所」這種事情往往只會把事情搞砸，大步走，但是不要跑。

Fidget, but not excessively. Gently shifting your legs and hips, and other subtle movements, interfere with urinating and help encourage your body to "wait". However, excessive or exaggerated fidgeting increase tension and pressure.
輕輕的坐立不安。輕輕把你的腿和臀部，和其他微妙的運動，干擾尿意並鼓勵你的身體繼續等待 。 然而，過分誇大或煩躁的舉動會增加緊張和壓力。
4.        Minimize drinking anything while you need to pee. But drink enough to maintain normal hydration. Avoid beverages, if holding pee occasionally for short periods.
Drink enough to stay hydrated, if holding pee for long periods, but avoid unnecessary beverages. Drink when you are actually thirsty, don't drink a beverage “just because it’s there”.
盡量減少飲用任何東西。但是至少要喝足夠的水來維持身體正常代謝作用。避免飲料，如果舉行撒尿偶爾短時間。 喝足夠的水份後，如果持有撒尿很長時間，但避免不必要的飲料。 飲料當您實際上是口渴了，不要喝飲料“只是因為它的存在。
5.        Avoid any thoughts about letting out pee. Do not to think of anything that may make you want to pee more. 避免想到任何更想尿出來的事情。
o        Do not think about using the bathroom, until you are actually there.
Do not think about trying to get to a bathroom, until access to one is likely to become available or allowed. Keep your mind away from words and images representing toilets. - Pictures of toilets/toilets seats, model bathrooms displays, different words for toilet, etc.
除非你真的抵達廁所了，否則不要想到上廁所這件事。別去想到跟廁所有關的字句或影像、圖片等等。
o        Repeatedly tell yourself you do not have to go. Give yourself reasons not to go.  反覆地告訴自己還不需要去上，替自己找一些還不用去的理由。
o        Know you cannot actually “burst” your bladder. Your body’s reflexes will prevent serious injury.
實際上膀胱不會真的「爆炸」。 身體的反射機制會防止這種嚴重的傷害。
So long as you are still able to "hold-back", your bladder is not in serious danger.
Feeling like you’re going “explode”, or in some other way hurt yourself, is an illusion.
Primitive instincts combined with increased discomfort when the bladder contracts.
The feeling will soon pass, and you can control the feeling till the contraction is over.

只要你有本事忍得住，那就代表膀胱還沒有承受到會帶來嚴重傷害的危險。感官上覺得快爆了，或是覺得會受傷其實是錯覺。當膀胱收縮時，不適感本來就是自然的，但是當膀胱的收縮活動結束後，會很快消逝過去。

6.        Stay Focused 注意別的事情
o        Use other things to distract you from your bladder. Do not let your bladder distract you from other things. 用別的事情把注意力轉移，而不是讓膀胱去轉移你的注意力。
o        Don't immediately think about going, when a restroom becomes available or permission is given. Remember first to hold it till you actually get to the restroom, then proceed to go there without being in a rush or hurry. This will reduce the sensation of your bladder "becoming excited" once the prospect of a restroom is near.
當上廁所的機會來臨時，別一下子就想著可以得到解放。切記必須堅持到真的進入廁所，而且並不會匆匆忙忙才可以。這樣會降低你的膀胱因為感覺得廁所將近而帶來的興奮。
o        Do not compromise if not absolutely sure you want to compromise. 不要妥協。
Remember your original reasons to not pee. 記得你本來就不打算尿尿的。
Determine if the situation might end soon. 先確定不能上廁所的狀況是否即將解除。
If it will - resolve to hold on till it does. 如果是的話，就堅持到解除後。
If not - resolve to hold on if there is any doubt about peeing in your mind. 如果沒辦法，千萬不能對尿尿有任何的懸念。
Don't "debate about peeing or not". Hold-it unless you are absolutely sure about letting go.  不必在心裡面猶豫著尿或不尿。 忍著，除非你已經做好決定要尿褲子了。

Being "unsure" or "debating" about "waiting to pee or not" are thoughts that make you want to pee more. 猶豫不決只會讓尿意更加旺盛。
They also mean you're not really sure if you want to pee.
Resolve to continue holding your urine when you are unsure. 不確定的話，就忍著吧。
Only change your mind if you're absolutely sure you want to.
7.        Resolve the situation so you can pee - on your terms. 在你允許的狀況下才可以尿尿。
Not someone else's terms, the situation's terms, or your bladder's terms. 不是別人允許你，也不是狀況允許你，更不是膀胱允許你。
o        Try to find a restroom once one is possible, if availability is the problem. But keep your mind off the idea when unlikely to be available.  試著在可能的時候趕快找到廁所，不過要是沒指望的話，那就不要再想了。
o        Try to get permission, if permission is being denied. Say it's an emergency. But avoid "crying wolf" (claiming an emergency every time) if the situation is one you are likely to encounter regularly.  如果提出上廁所的要求被拒絕的話，再求情看看吧。就說這次狀況非常緊急。不過別當那個喊著狼來了的小孩。

[edit] Tips 技巧
&#8226;        Easier ways to hold your pee are, if you're standing sucking your stomach in (not too much),if you're sitting bending forward a bit, and if you're walking, don't stop walking; keep moving. Those ways relieve the pressure and aren't that noticeable.  
&#8226;        If there is no possible way or place to pee, roll up a towel or something and pee on that, to avoid peeing in your pants.
&#8226;        Apply the steps described in a sensible way. They are most effective when done naturally and there is no need to feel embarrassed about the issue. Over exaggeration or "acting out" can make the situation worse and increase tension on the bladder. But trying to hide or disguise your situation can interfere with asserting control.
&#8226;        Maintain a light, firm, positive attitude. Project the belief you can hold pee in as long as you want.
o        It's important to understand every thought, feeling, and impulse affects the behavior of your body.
So does what you project or believe you project to others, and the response you perceive in return.
Thoughts are things, patterns of energy which affect both our behavior and our body's reflexes.
o        Project the concept of being successfully able to hold your bladder.
In a way believable to your own mind, and convincing to any would be observers.
Be not "desperate", but not "in denial" about the urge.
o        Apply the effort needed to hold it in, but no more than this.
Acting like control of your pee requires more effort, falsely adds to your own sense of needing to pee.
Excessive "struggling" and fidgeting encourages thoughts of not being in control.
"Running to the bathroom" implies being "near your limit" and "unable to wait", and tells your body subconsciously you "will reach a toilet sooner" and "need to go right now".
o        And, no less effort either.
Limiting the effort to control pee interferes with actions used to hold it.
"Hiding" the urge to pee creates tension, feels silly, suggests immaturity, and can imply that you're control is only "an act".
o        Acknowledge having an urge to pee which you are in complete command over.
Project this state by acting accordingly. Don't try to hide it, but don't exaggerate it.
Personalities vary. So it's fine to either let others know or not, which ever best suggests control of your bodily functions at the time.
o        It is an "urge" to pee, not a "need" to pee.
Except if asking for permission to use the restroom, consider your situation as you have/feel an urge but don't need to go yet. Make it clear (especially to yourself) that you can wait.
&#8226;        When having to hold it happens often, do not habitually restrict fluids to reduce pee.
o        Dehydration causes urine to become concentrated - irritating the bladder and increasing the "urge" to let go. Regularly avoiding beverages to reduce pee may lead to infection. Making long term bladder control more difficult.(see Warnings)
o        Drink enough to maintain hydration, only unnecessary beverages should be avoided.
You should be able to do this and still “hold-the-water”.
o        Drink small amounts frequently, not large amounts at once.
Drinking large amount of water at once causes the excess water to end up in your bladder. Use small amounts at a time to maintain hydration while drinking less.
o        Choose water or hydration drinks.
Avoid caffeine, alcohol, and sugary drinks unless you have a specific reason to drink them.
Hydration drinks and water hydrate you better with smaller amounts than sugary drinks.
Alcohol and caffeine cause dehydration and make you pee more at the same time.
o        Practice "Kegel" exercises.
These where designed to improve continence by strengthening pelvic floor muscles. The muscles used to hold it. Although intended to help incontinent people, they can give anyone extra control to hold it longer.

[edit] Warnings
&#8226;        Holding your urine, when done too often, can cause a build up of bacteria in your urinary tract and lead to possible infection.
These instructions are intended to aid in holding urine only when the situation calls for it.

To reduce associated risks
"Flush" your urinary tract between times of having, or choosing, to hold pee by drinking plenty of fluids when not holding pee.
If you expect to hold your pee back often, drinking cranberry juice has been shown to reduce the associated risks.
&#8226;        Avoiding fluids to reduce urine is unnecessary and harmful, and can cause dehydration and heat injury (if in a hot environment)
Dehydration causes urine to become more concentrated, may increase the likelihood of bacteria and infection, and may be counter productive as concentrated urine can increase feelings of urgency.

[edit] Myths
Myth - Tycho Brahe died by holding his bladder out of politeness till it burst.
&#8226;        Origin - Tycho Brahe's death occurred in the 1600's, before modern medicine understood why you cannot burst your bladder this way. The original 1600's diagnosis was that a "stone" kept him from passing urine, later his "bladder burst". He was never diagnosed with death by trying to holding pee too long. Kepler recounted that during a banquet 5 days before Brahe's death, Brahe didn't want to empty his bladder and would wait until he got home; later he was unable to do so. There is no evidence politeness or social protocol was a reason for waiting; difficulty passing urine was more likely the reason. Also, the problem was that he could not pass urine after getting home, not that he held too long.
&#8226;        Truth - In 1991, scientists found that Tycho actually died of mercury poisoning. Tycho's symptoms—unable to pee, blood in urine, fever, delirium—are symptoms of mercury poisoning. The original diagnosis of "burst bladder by stone" is now known to not be medically possible. The kidneys and ureters cannot produce enough pressure to burst the bladder. When there is an obstruction, the kidneys fail first. In the case of trying to hold it, the body would protect the kidneys by reacting violently, forcing the person to wet themselves.
Myth - Jennifer Strange died from holding in her pee, in the "Hold your wee for a Wii" contest.
&#8226;        Origin - In January of 2007, Jennifer Strange died after the "Hold your wee for a Wii" contest, from drinking a glass of water every ten minutes while trying to hold her pee as long as she could, in order to win a Wii game console.
&#8226;        Truth - Jennifer Strange died from drinking too much water in the "Hold your wee for a Wii" contest. Holding her pee had nothing to do with it. Her death was caused by hyponatremia, which occurs when electrolytes are washed out by drinking too much water. It does not matter if you hold the pee or not: when you drink too much water, the electrolytes and water both end up leaving the blood.
